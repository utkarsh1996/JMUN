About JMUN

Model United Nations (MUN) is a simulation of United Nations Organization in form and content. It aims to create awareness and educate participants about current events and topics of international concern. The delegates convene together to debate and discuss the agenda at hand in each committee. There lies an opportunity of interacting with hundreds of highly motivated students from diverse backgrounds for the two day summit. While simulating committees of the United Nations and other international bodies, the delegates develop and practice skills in public speaking, group communication, research, policy analysis, active listening, negotiation, conflict resolution, note taking, and technical writing. And in the end, they come away with a greater understanding of the world's problems and the diverse perspectives.
We believe that JMUN will be an extraordinary learning experience for those wishing to take part in it. It’s time to redefine and change, change to achieve a prosperous and peaceful world and JMUN is providing you an opportunity to be recognized!

Arab League

The legacy of Jaypee Model United Nations brings to you the simulation of the rarest of the committees, 'Arab League'. Witness every edge of knowledge, experience and diplomacy required in one of the most war-torn regions of the world. Arab League is a regional organisation of Arab countries, currently having 22 members and 4 observer states. In the present scenario, the effectiveness of Arab League has been hampered by division of the member states. Whenever there have been hostilities between traditional monarchies - such as Saudi Arabia, Jordan and Morocco - and new republics, or revolutionary states such as Egypt under Gamal Abdel Nasser, Ba'athist Syria and Iraq, and Libya under Muammar Gaddafi or even when the league was severely tested by the US-led attack on Saddam Hussein's Iraq, with some backing the war, some opposing it and others standing on the sidelines, has crippled the league in the sphere of high politics because decisions made by the Arab League are binding only on members who voted for them. Therefore Arab League plays an instrumental role in maintaining world peace. We are proud to simulate this committee. Come and witness the versatility of JMUN '18 !

Council of Europe

Justice is another name of liberty, equality and fraternity. - B.R. Ambedkar The Council of Europe is one of the most momentous organisations of the world. It is an international organization that parallels the European Union. It focuses on democracy, human rights and cultural exchange, and is best known for the European Convention on Human Rights. It has its own institutions, including a Committee of Ministers and a Parliamentary Assembly, and, of course, the European Court of Human Rights. The Council of Europe has 47 member States and is headquartered in Strasbourg, France. Jaypee Model United Nations is indeed very delighted to introduce you all to the 'Council of Europe' - a committee which fosters justice and righteousness to protect the rule of law and promote peaceful co-operation.

Lok Sabha

Democracy must be built through open societies that share information. When there is information, there is enlightenment. When there is debate, there are solutions. When there is no sharing of power, no rule of law, no accountability, there is abuse, corruption, subjugation and indignation. Are you one of them who loves to debate incessantly and be a part of prolonged contention? If yes, then this edition of the Jaypee Model United Nations has the perfect committee simulated for you - The Historic Lok Sabha Committee. Also known as the House of the people, Lok Sabha is the lower house of the Indian Parliament which plays a substantial role in the legislative process of the largest democracy of the world. All its members are directly elected by the citizens of the country and its present membership stands at 545, out of these 523 are elected by the people of all Indian States and 20 by the people of the Union Territories. With the Historical Lok Sabha Committee, be a part of the legacy to speak your heart out and take a stand for what you believe in, even if it means standing alone.

United Nations General Assembly

A genuine leader is not a searcher of consensus but a moulder of consensus. In the spirit of empowering people and raising leaders who shape the future, we present to you United Nations General Assembly. The first ever UN General Assembly was convened in 1946 in London and witnessed participation of 51 countries. At present, they are 193 member nations each of which has its own vote. The General Assembly (GA) is the main deliberative, policymaking and representative body of the UN. The concerns of this body of UN range from international peace and security, budget, and election or suspension of members from the council. The aim behind the first committee being the UN General Assembly is to search for leaders who have the ability to mould the consensus and bring about great waves of change

United Nations Human Rights Council

To deny people their human rights is to challenge their very humanity -Nelson Mandela. The 8th edition of Jaypee Model United Nations brings to you a forum and a springboard for action against the violation of human rights with the simulation of the committee 'Human Rights Council'. The Human Rights Council is an inter-governmental body within the United Nations system made up of 47 States responsible for the promotion and protection of all human rights around the globe. The council works closely with the Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights (OHCHR) and also addresses important thematic human rights issues such as freedom of association and assembly, freedom of expression, freedom of belief and religion, women's rights, LGBT rights, and the rights of racial and ethnic minorities.
